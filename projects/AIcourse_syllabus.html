Applied Artificial Intelligence for Business Implementation

Overview

ITM TBD: Applied Artificial Intelligence for Business Implementation is an elective course designed for MIS and MSIS graduate students at the University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa, Shidler College of Business. The course bridges the gap between AI theory and enterprise practice, equipping students with the conceptual and applied skills needed to lead AI initiatives within real organizations.

Modern enterprises increasingly rely on AI-driven systems to optimize workflows, enhance decision-making, and generate measurable business value. This course prepares students to evaluate, plan, and implement AI solutions — not just as technologists, but as strategic business leaders.

Course Objectives

Students who complete this course will be able to:

  • Align AI with business goals — Evaluate and communicate how AI initiatives connect to operational workflows, financial outcomes, and long-term organizational transformation
  • Explain core AI concepts — Articulate machine learning, neural networks, tokenization, and agentic AI with an emphasis on practical business applications
  • Apply real-world case studies — Examine documented AI implementations, cloud-based tools, and measurable ROI outcomes from industry
  • Manage data requirements — Identify data labeling, taxonomy design, pipelines, feature engineering, vector databases, and synthetic data needs for AI projects
  • Develop AI governance — Write organizational AI policies covering acceptable use, risk management, data stewardship, and regulatory compliance

Course Structure

The course runs 15 weeks and covers the following topic progression:

Week Topics

2 Core AI concepts: ML, neural networks, tokenization, generative AI

Major Assignments

Applied AI Strategy and Implementation Plan (30%)

The signature project of the course. Student groups of 3–5 are randomly assigned a simulated business and tasked with producing a complete, theoretical AI implementation plan. The deliverable includes:

  • A 10-page written report detailing strategy, infrastructure, data requirements, and projected ROI
  • A 20-minute group presentation summarizing findings and recommendations
  • An AI policy document tailored to the business’s regulatory and ethical context
  • Peer reviews assessing each group member’s contributions

5-Minute Presentations (10%)

Each student selects a current AI news article, business case, or academic paper and presents it to the class in five minutes with up to five PowerPoint slides. This keeps students current in a fast-moving field and develops communication skills around technical topics.

Exams (35%)

Both the midterm and final exams are closed book, closed note, handwritten, and in-person — no AI tools permitted. Exams assess conceptual understanding, practical application, and key course material.

In-Class Exercises & Participation (20%)

Hands-on activities using cloud platforms, discussion posts, and occasional quizzes reinforce course content throughout the semester.

Generative AI Policy

Students are encouraged to use generative AI tools for most assignments in this course — after all, it’s an AI course. However, the standard is high: submissions must demonstrate critical thinking and meaningful engagement with the topic. AI-generated content that is verbose, superficial, or low-quality will not meet course standards.

Curriculum Design Philosophy

Given the rapidly evolving nature of AI, no traditional textbook was selected for this course — any static text would be outdated within months. Instead, course materials are drawn from:

  • Peer-reviewed academic research
  • Reputable news and media articles
  • Industry case studies and technical reports (e.g., McKinsey, MIT)

This ensures students are always working with the most current knowledge in the field.

Impact

This course reflects a growing need in graduate business education to produce professionals who can lead AI adoption — not just understand it. By integrating technical literacy, strategic thinking, and responsible AI governance into a single course, students graduate prepared to drive real organizational change in an AI-first world.
